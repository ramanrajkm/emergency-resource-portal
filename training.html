1. Earthquakes

Training focuses on structural safety and immediate “Drop, Cover, and Hold on” techniques.

Preparedness Training:

  • Building Assessment: Identify and repair deep plaster cracks in ceilings or foundations.
  • Retrofitting: Professionals are trained in Seismic Retrofitting to strengthen existing buildings.
  • Home Safety: Secure heavy furniture and overhead lighting to prevent them from falling.

Response Guidelines:

  • Indoors: Stay inside; move away from windows and glass.
  • Outdoors: Move to an open area away from power lines and buildings.

2. Floods

Training emphasizes early warning systems and safe evacuation procedures.

Preparedness Training:

  • Community Mapping: Volunteers like Aapda Mitras identify safe zones and evacuation routes.
  • Emergency Kits: Families maintain kits with food, water, and essential documents in waterproof containers.

Response Guidelines:

  • Water Safety: Never walk or drive through moving water; 6 inches can knock a person down.
  • Health: Use only boiled or chlorinated water to avoid waterborne diseases.

3. Cyclones

Guidelines focus on coastal community resilience and infrastructure protection.

Preparedness Training:

  • Shelter Management: Training for local officials on Multi-Purpose Cyclone Shelters (MPCS).
  • Home Securing: Teach residents to secure loose tiles and repair doors/windows before the season.

Response Guidelines:

  • "Eye" Warning: Do not venture out if winds calm down; the "eye" of the storm may pass.
  • Communication: Use SMS for emergency updates to save battery and bandwidth.

4. Fires (Urban & Forest)

Training prioritizes a culture of preparedness and standardized response equipment.

Preparedness Training:

  • Fire Services Scaling: Guidelines for state fire services on scaling and training.
  • Forest Fire Prevention: Training for farmers to avoid burning stubble near forests.

Response Guidelines:

  • Immediate Action: Keep emergency contact numbers for the district fire service handy.
  • Evacuation: Follow fire exit signs; never use elevators during a fire.

5. Landslides

Training is critical for hilly regions, focusing on hazard mapping and soil stability.

Preparedness Training:

  • Technical Assistance: Training for professionals on landslide mitigation and DPR preparation.
  • Site Selection: Avoid construction on slopes where soil layers dip outward.

Response Guidelines:

  • Rapid Movement: Move away from the path or downstream valleys immediately if a landslide occurs.
  • Awareness: Listen to weather updates before travelling to hilly regions.
